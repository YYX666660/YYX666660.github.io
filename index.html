Location-Guided Audio-Visual Spatial Audio Separation


Yuxin Ye      Wenming Yang      Yapeng Tian      





Abstract:

Audio-visual separation (AVS) is one of the most indispensable applications for multidimensional human perception, with its performance separating specific sounds from the corresponding objects. Recent studies have achieved promising audio-visual separation performance on monaural sounds but remained blank space of spatial audio, which contains abundant spatial information of sound sources corresponding to visual locations of sounding objects in space. Limitations of MAVS further hinder listeners from exploiting the 3D spatial position information to resolve spatial audio ambiguity caused by multiple sources. To alleviate these issues, we propose LAVSS: a location-guided audio-visual spatial audio separator. Our approach starts with pre-training on an external mono dataset to perform network transfer learning, which is the first attempt to demonstrate the correlation of mono and binaural channels. To further exploit the spatial properties of stereo, we leverage the positional representations of sounding objects fused with the visual modality, which consistently guides spatial audio separation. We validate the performance of LAVSS on Fair-Play and compare it against the SOTA baseline for MAVS, demonstrating our superiorities and thus constituting a new benchmark.
