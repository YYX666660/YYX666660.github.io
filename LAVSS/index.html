Location-Guided Audio-Visual Spatial Audio Separation


Yuxin Ye      Wenming Yang      Yapeng Tian      





Abstract:

Recent deep learning approaches have achieved impressive performance on monaural audio-visual separation (MAVS) tasks. However, these methods devised for mono audios are deficient in perceiving the virtual and augmented reality scenarios surrounded by spatial audios, especially in cases where listeners require an immersive experience. To alleviate this issue, we propose LAVSS: a location-guided audio-visual spatial audio separator. Specifically, we explicitly exploit positional representations of sounding objects as an additional spatial guidance for separating individual binaural sounds, considering that binaural audio contains abundant spatial information of sound sources corresponding to visual locations of sounding objects. In addition, we adopt separator pre-training to transfer knowledge from rich mono sounds to boost spatial audio separation performance, demonstrating the correlation between monaural and binaural channels. Experiments on Fair-Play dataset can demonstrate the superiority and generalization ability of our LAVSS over existing state-of-the-art audio-visual separation approaches.
